About Me

Driven by the Earth's complexity

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Civil & Construction Engineering at Oregon State University, under the supervision of Professor Dr. Ben Leshchinsky.

My work is driven by a fascination with how the earth moves and behaves under pressure — specifically focusing on the complex mechanics that govern soil stability and subsurface environments.

As a researcher, I operate at the intersection of high-precision laboratory testing and computational modeling. I specialize in translating raw field data into robust numerical simulations — whether running iterative solvers to predict fluid movement in soil or using MATLAB to analyze complex signals.

My goal is to turn experimental observations into clear, actionable insights that advance our understanding of geotechnical systems.

What I Do

Areas of Research

01
Geotechnical Engineering
Investigating soil behavior under static and dynamic loads, with emphasis on subsurface stability and failure mechanisms.
02
Engineering Geology
Applying geological principles to civil engineering problems, bridging the gap between earth science and structural analysis.
03
Geological Mapping
Field-based characterization of geological formations and structural features to inform engineering design.
04
Geophysical Analysis
Utilizing seismic and non-invasive subsurface methods to characterize soil and rock conditions in the field.
05
Foundation Engineering
Design and analysis of foundation systems to safely transfer structural loads to the underlying soil or rock.
06
Slope Stability
Numerical and experimental analysis of slopes under various loading conditions, including seismic and pore pressure effects.
